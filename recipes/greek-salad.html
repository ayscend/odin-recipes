What is a Greek Salad

Greek Salad or Horiatiki, meaning village salad, originated in Athens, Greece. This salad consists of various vegetables excluding lettuce, which can be a bonus to some. Greek salad is known for using feta cheese, since in the past there were price caps on regular salads set by the government in the 1960's. To bypass this, it would be added in the salad to make it more expensive and profitable as this made it a new dish.

Ingredients for Greek Salad

  • 1 english cucumber(large cucumber)
  • Half a red onion
  • 1/3 cup kalamata olives
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 1 bell pepper
  • 7 oz feta cheese

    • Dressing Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • Zest of half a lemon
  • 1 tbsp fresh chopped thyme
  • 2 tbsp fresh chopped oregano
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp honey mustard
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp salt

Steps

  1. Get a bowl and a mason jar.
  2. Cut cucumber length-wise, and chop into semi-circles.
  3. Chop red onion in thin slices.
  4. Chop kalamata olives in halves, to avoid seeds.
  5. Chop tomatoes in bite-sized pieces.
  6. Chop bell pepper in thin pieces.

    7. Now time for the dressing!

  7. Add the olive oil into the mason jar, along with the lemon zest.
  8. Add chopped fresh thyme and oregano into the jar.
  9. Add red wine vinegar, honey mustard, and lemon juice.
  10. Salt to taste, then shake it up in the jar!

    11. Back to the Salad

  11. Drizzle the dressing all over the veggies after combined in the bowl.
  12. Lastly, add in all the feta cheese, crumble it up and mix in with your hands.
  13. Enjoy your fresh Greek Salad!
